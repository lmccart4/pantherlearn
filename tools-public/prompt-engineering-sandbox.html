The CRAFT Framework

Every great prompt has five ingredients. Use CRAFT to build prompts that give you exactly what you need.

C
Context Set the scene. Give the AI the background it needs. Who is the audience? What's the situation? What do you already know?
R
Role Assign the AI a role. "You are a history tutor," "Act as a debate coach," or "You are a science journalist." This shapes the expertise and perspective of the response.
A
Action Tell the AI exactly what to do. Be specific: "Explain," "Compare," "Generate a list of," "Write a paragraph that..." The clearer the action, the better the output.
F
Format How should the output look? Bullet points? A 5-paragraph essay? A table? A numbered list? Specify length, structure, and organization.
T
Tone What's the vibe? Formal, casual, encouraging, academic, humorous? Tone controls the voice and style of the response.

Example: CRAFT in Action

Weak prompt:

"Tell me about photosynthesis"

CRAFT prompt:

[Context] I'm a 9th grader studying for a biology test on cellular processes. [Role] Act as a patient biology tutor. [Action] Explain the two stages of photosynthesis (light reactions and Calvin cycle) and how they connect. [Format] Use a numbered list with simple analogies for each stage, then a 2-sentence summary. [Tone] Keep it friendly and easy to understand, like you're explaining to a friend.
