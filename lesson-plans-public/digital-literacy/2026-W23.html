Monday • Lesson 1
Gaming Project: Refine & Review
March 9, 2026
Do Now
Students review their learning objectives for the day — giving constructive peer feedback, applying that feedback to improve their own video, and self-assessing against the project rubric. They answer a quick poll about how complete their video currently is.
Lesson Overview
0-5 min
Warm Up & Objectives: Students review learning goals and respond to the Question of the Day: “Why is getting feedback from someone else essential — even when you think your project is already good?”
5-20 min
Peer Review Protocol: Students pair up and follow a structured review — watch their partner's video twice (once silently, once with notes), then share specific, constructive feedback on strengths and areas for improvement
20-25 min
Self-Assessment: Students rate themselves on the video quality checklist covering content accuracy, editing, audio, hook, and overall polish
25-38 min
Polish Time: Students apply peer feedback and self-assessment results to refine transitions, audio levels, and pacing using WeVideo
38-40 min
Wrap Up: Students export their video and prepare for tomorrow's Showcase Day
Exit Ticket
Students identify the single most important improvement they still need to make before tomorrow's showcase, ensuring they have a clear focus for any remaining work time.
Tuesday • Lesson 2
Gaming Project: Showcase Day!
March 10, 2026
Do Now
Students do a final check on their video completion status and ensure all three deliverables are exported and ready for presentation. They review objectives for the showcase: presenting their work, providing peer feedback, and reflecting on the project.
Lesson Overview
0-5 min
Final Prep: Students confirm their video is exported, review the showcase format, and prepare their quick pitch about what their video covers
5-20 min
Peer Review Round: Students pair up for one final feedback exchange, noting the strongest part of their partner's video and one specific suggestion for improvement
20-25 min
Self-Assessment: Students complete the video quality checklist and rate their work against the project rubric
25-38 min
Gallery Walk & Showcase: Students present their gaming documentaries, view classmates' work, and note what they appreciated about others' presentations
38-40 min
Unit Reflection: Students reflect on skills learned throughout the Gaming & Esports unit and what they'd do differently with more time
Exit Ticket
Students complete a final reflection identifying the most important improvement they made during the project and what skills from this unit they'll carry forward.
