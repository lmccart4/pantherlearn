Tuesday
Finding Your Niche — The Riches Are in the Niches
April 21, 2026
Digital Entrepreneurship
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.1, ISTE 1.2
  • Define 'niche' and explain why specificity beats breadth in digital business
  • Use the 3-circle framework (passion + skill + demand) to evaluate niche ideas
  • Build a personal niche tree narrowing a broad topic to a specific, viable business
Do Now / Warm Up
"Cooking videos" — there are 50 million of them on YouTube. You'd compete with every cooking channel ever made. "5-ingredient weeknight dinners for high school students who only have a microwave" — now you have maybe 50 competitors, and a clearly defined audience. Go ahead and win them.
Key Activities
  • Warm Up
  • The 3-Circle Framework
  • Build Your Niche Tree
  • Wrap Up
  • Key Vocabulary
Exit Ticket
Exit Ticket: What's your final niche statement for this unit? What's one piece of demand evidence you found that convinces you this niche has real potential?

Thursday
Branding 101 — Name, Logo, Colors, Vibe
April 23, 2026
Digital Entrepreneurship
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.1, ISTE 1.2
  • Identify the 4 pillars of brand identity (name, logo, colors, voice)
  • Analyze how branding decisions communicate values and attract specific audiences
  • Build a shareable brand identity kit for their business in Canva
Do Now / Warm Up
You've never bought from a particular brand before. You've never heard a review. But you look at their Instagram and think — *this feels legit, I'd buy from them.* That feeling isn't random. It's the result of a hundred design decisions: colors, fonts, photos, tone, spacing. That's branding.
Key Activities
  • Warm Up
  • The 4 Pillars of Brand Identity
  • Build Your Brand Identity Kit
  • Wrap Up
  • Key Vocabulary
Exit Ticket
Exit Ticket: Look at your brand page. Would your target audience trust this brand based on how it looks? What would you change if you had another hour?
Friday
Revenue Models — How Does the Money Actually Work?
April 24, 2026
Digital Entrepreneurship
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.1, ISTE 1.2
  • Identify and explain 5 common digital revenue models
  • Calculate realistic revenue projections using basic assumptions
  • Audit 6 fictional businesses to see how hidden fees change real take-home pay
  • Select a revenue model for their own business and justify it with math
Do Now / Warm Up
Two creators. Same topic. Same content quality. **Creator A** has 50,000 followers and makes $0. **Creator B** has 800 followers and makes $2,400/month. What's the difference? It's not about size — it's about how they get paid.
Key Activities
  • Warm Up
  • 5 Revenue Models for Digital Businesses
  • Revenue Autopsy
  • Wrap Up
  • Key Vocabulary
Exit Ticket
Exit Ticket: What surprised you most about revenue math for digital businesses? Did any of the 6 autopsies change how you think about your own model?
