Four Questions Courtroom — Scenario Deck
Scenario 1
A hospital uses AI to decide which ER patients get seen first. It has been wrong 3 times this year about priority.
Scenario 2
A school district buys an AI to grade essays at scale. It flags 8% as likely AI-written. Appeals require proving a negative.
Scenario 3
A city deploys facial recognition at every major intersection. Court records show 1 wrongful arrest per month.
Scenario 4
An AI denies 40% of mortgage applications in one zip code. The bank says the model is confidential.
Scenario 5
A resume-sorting AI downgrades applicants who mention “women’s college.” The company calls it pattern-matching.
Scenario 6
An AI tutor promises equal access — but only runs well on devices less than 3 years old.
Scenario 7
A federal agency uses an AI to predict visa overstays. The model includes country-of-origin as a feature.
Scenario 8
An AI content moderator removes 20% of posts from activists in one country. Posts in other countries aren’t flagged.
Scenario 9
A music-generation AI writes a top-10 hit using training data drawn from 50,000 uncredited songwriters.
Scenario 10
Your insurance company uses AI to decide whether your chronic condition qualifies for coverage. There is no human reviewer.
Scenario 11
A court uses an AI risk score to set bail. Defendants cannot see the score, the formula, or the training data.
Scenario 12
An AI translates school announcements from English to Spanish. Parents report it says the wrong dates and wrong grades.
Scenario 13
A factory uses AI to predict worker injury risk. Workers flagged “high risk” are quietly passed over for hiring.
Scenario 14
An AI predicts which students are likely to drop out so counselors can “intervene.” Flagged students report feeling profiled.
Scenario 15
A utility company uses AI to decide which neighborhoods get power restored first after an outage.
Scenario 16
An X-ray AI outperforms 9 of 10 radiologists — trained entirely on adult patients. It is now being used to screen children.
Scenario 17
A platform’s algorithm pushes eating-disorder content to teens who linger on workout videos — by design.
Scenario 18
A startup sells an AI that generates personalized political ads for 50 cents each. No disclosure required.
Scenario 19
A school district’s AI scans classroom cameras and flags students who “look disengaged.” Teachers see the reports.
Scenario 20
A generative AI recreates the voice of a deceased musician for a new album. The family is split on whether it should exist.
