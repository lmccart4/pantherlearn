Demystifying AI — Presentation Rubric

AI Literacy Assessment — Mr. McCarthy

Total Points 30 pts
Criteria Refining (100%) Developing (85%) Approaching (65%)
Article Selection & Claim Identification
Refining
Clearly identifies the article and its specific misleading claim. The claim is quoted or paraphrased accurately.
Developing
Identifies the article but the specific claim is vague or not clearly stated.
Approaching
Article choice is unclear or claim is missing/incorrect.
Evidence & Fact-Checking (2+ facts)
Refining
Provides 2+ accurate, specific facts that directly counter the article's claim. Evidence is well-chosen and convincing.
Developing
Provides 1-2 facts but they are vague, partially relevant, or not clearly connected to the claim.
Approaching
Facts are missing, inaccurate, or don't address the claim.
Why It Matters
Refining
Clearly explains why the misconception is harmful and who could be affected. Shows real-world understanding.
Developing
Mentions that the misconception matters but reasoning is surface-level or generic.
Approaching
No explanation of impact, or explanation doesn't connect to the misconception.
Verdict & Critical Thinking
Refining
Delivers a clear verdict on the article's trustworthiness. Explains what a critical reader should do differently.
Developing
Verdict is present but reasoning is thin or doesn't connect to the evidence presented.
Approaching
No clear verdict, or verdict contradicts the evidence shown.
Presentation Design
Refining
4-6 slides. Clean, visual design. Short bullets (not paragraphs). Easy to follow.
Developing
Has slides but design is cluttered, text-heavy, or hard to follow. Needs polish.
Approaching
Fewer than 4 slides, minimal effort, or content is mostly copy-pasted text walls.

Grading Scale

Refining30 / 30 pts (100%)
Developing25.5 / 30 pts (85%)
Approaching19.5 / 30 pts (65%)
Not Submitted0 / 30 pts
