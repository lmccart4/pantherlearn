Weekly Teaching Reflection — Week 18
"The students responded well last week to more bite-sized activities. Looking to break down and improve on that this week by maintaining focused, manageable tasks within each lesson while building toward a cohesive final product."
Week at a Glance
Students will explore the debate around social media and mental health, then learn Canva design principles to create an evidence-based awareness campaign. The week progresses from research and stance-taking to hands-on design practice, culminating in a 3-piece campaign portfolio and peer gallery walk.
Monday • Lesson 1
The Debate
February 9, 2026
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.1
Do Now
Students reflect on their personal social media usage: which platforms they use, how much time they spend, and how it makes them feel. This honest self-assessment sets the foundation for evidence-based learning.
Lesson Overview
0-5 min
Opening Context: Review headlines and statistics about social media's impact on youth mental health, including the U.S. Surgeon General's 2023 advisory
5-10 min
Quick-Write: Students complete honest self-reflection about their relationship with social media on handout
10-25 min
Evidence Hunt: Students research and document 2 pieces of evidence for BOTH sides of the debate (harmful vs. helpful), promoting balanced critical thinking
25-35 min
Take a Stance: Based on evidence and personal experience, students position themselves on the spectrum and explain their reasoning
35-40 min
Share Out: Volunteers share their stances with reasoning; introduce week-long campaign project
Exit Ticket
Students preview the week ahead: they'll learn Canva and create a social media awareness campaign about mental health, taking whatever stance they believe in backed by evidence.

Tuesday • Lesson 2
Canva Basics
February 10, 2026
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.5
Do Now
Students navigate to canva.com and sign in using their school Google accounts, ensuring all students have access to the design tool before instruction begins.
Lesson Overview
0-5 min
Introduction: What is Canva and why professionals use it; overview of what can be created with the platform
5-15 min
Interface Tour: Guided walkthrough of left sidebar (templates, elements, text, uploads), center canvas workspace, and top toolbar (fonts, colors, alignment)
15-20 min
Key Tools Demo: Demonstrate text, elements, photos, colors, and layers with pro tips (templates, font limits, alignment guides, undo shortcuts)
20-35 min
Practice Task: Students create "Social Media & Me" post with background color, their name, and at least one shape/icon (Instagram Post square format)
35-40 min
Export & Submit: Students download design as PNG/JPG and check off completion items on handout
Exit Ticket
Students identify one Canva feature they want to explore more in the next lesson, encouraging reflection and self-directed learning goals.

Wednesday • Lesson 3
Design Principles
February 11, 2026
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.5
Do Now
Students consider why design matters by comparing bad vs. good design outcomes (scrolling past vs. grabbing attention; message lost vs. communicated clearly).
Lesson Overview
0-5 min
Hook: Why design matters—you have 3 seconds to grab attention on social media; contrast bad vs. good design outcomes
5-10 min
Visual Hierarchy: Teach how size, contrast, and position guide the viewer's eye to what matters most
10-15 min
Color & Contrast: Explore 2-3 color palettes, high contrast for readability, and mood matching with do's and don'ts
15-20 min
Typography & White Space: Cover font limits, size guidelines, readability, alignment, and the power of empty space
20-35 min
Design Analysis Activity: In pairs, students analyze example designs using the 4 principles and document observations on handout
35-40 min
Campaign Planning: Students begin planning their campaign by selecting stance, colors, mood/vibe, and main message
Exit Ticket
Students complete the "My Campaign Planning" section on their handout, identifying their stance (harmful/helpful/it depends), color choices, desired mood, and main message for tomorrow's creation day.

Thursday • Lesson 4
Campaign Creation
February 12, 2026
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.1, 8.1.8.DA.5
Do Now
Students review their campaign plan from Day 3 (stance, message, colors, facts) to ensure they're ready to begin creating.
Lesson Overview
0-5 min
Assignment Overview: Explain the 3-piece campaign (Instagram Post, Instagram Story, Infographic) and requirements (clear stance, 2+ facts, consistent design, name on each)
5-10 min
Success Criteria: Review design principles application and provide strategic tips (start with Instagram Post, pick colors first, use templates)
10-35 min
Work Time: Students create their 3-piece campaign in Canva with time checks at 15 min, 5 min, and 1 min remaining; teacher circulates for support
35-40 min
Progress Check: Students complete progress tracker on handout and note what still needs finishing tomorrow
Exit Ticket
Students identify what they still need to finish tomorrow, ensuring they have a clear plan for Friday's wrap-up and presentation.

Friday • Lesson 5
Share & Celebrate
February 13, 2026
NJSLS 8.1.8.DA.1
Do Now
Students complete any unfinished campaign pieces and verify all three designs (Instagram Post, Story, Infographic) are exported as PNG files and ready for submission.
Lesson Overview
0-10 min
Final Work Time: Students finish any incomplete pieces and export all designs
10-15 min
Submission: Students verify submission checklist (3 PNG exports, all handouts from Days 1-5) and upload everything to Google Classroom
15-30 min
Gallery Walk: Students display their campaigns around the classroom and walk around noting what they liked about 4 different classmates' presentations
30-40 min
Week 1 Reflection: Students complete reflection prompts about design skills learned, how their opinion evolved, and what they'd do differently with more time
Exit Ticket
Students complete a three-part reflection: identifying the design skill they're most proud of learning, describing how their opinion on social media & mental health changed (or stayed the same), and articulating what they would do differently with more time.
